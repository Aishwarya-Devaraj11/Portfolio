GENERATION OF ELECTRICITY FROM NOISE

USING BLOCKCHAIN

ABSTRACT

The need for an alternative source of energy is rising fast. Until now, the majority of the world's power needs rely upon the exploitation of non-renewable fossil fuels. These fossil fuels act as a by-supplier in electricity generation. However, since they are finite, there's an increasing compulsion to shift towards renewable sources.

Renewable energy sources include wind, heat, solar, tides, and even noise. These alternatives help generate electricity without producing harmful emissions, making them environmentally sustainable. Among them, noise is an often-ignored but abundant resource in our increasingly urbanized environments.

This project aims to convert the decibel levels of unwanted noise into useful electrical energy — achieving two goals: acting as a sustainable energy source and indicating local noise levels using a microphone and ESP8266 microcontroller.

The system uses a piezoelectric sensor to convert sound vibrations (pressure waves) into electrical energy. This energy flows through a bridge rectifier and a capacitor, then gets stored in a lithium-ion battery for later use. Meanwhile, the device also measures and displays decibel levels (dB), uploading the data in real-time to an IoT platform called “ThingSpeak” using ESP8266.

Key Highlights:

  • Sustainable, noise-powered energy generation
  • Real-time noise level monitoring via decibel display
  • Wireless data transmission through ESP8266 to ThingSpeak
  • Energy stored in a lithium-ion battery using rectification
  • Highly cost-effective and scalable for public use

This low-cost device can be securely placed in high-noise environments like airports, factories, and traffic intersections — not only to produce electricity but also to monitor and indicate environmental noise levels. Urban areas, hospitals, and residential zones can use this system to track sound pollution and take necessary action to mitigate it.

In summary, this project proposes a dual-function, sustainable solution that harnesses ambient noise to generate clean energy while simultaneously helping to manage sound pollution — a powerful contribution to both energy and environmental management.

EXISTING SYSTEM

In the current energy generation landscape, the primary sources include fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas), hydropower, nuclear energy, and renewable sources like solar and wind. While there has been significant progress in utilizing renewable sources, ambient energy forms such as sound or noise remain largely untapped.

There are a few experimental systems and research efforts that explore converting sound energy into electrical energy using piezoelectric materials. However, these efforts are:

  • Limited in scale and efficiency
  • Not integrated into a broader energy distribution framework
  • Lacking real-time monitoring and transaction validation mechanisms

Additionally, traditional energy management systems face the following challenges:

  • Lack of transparency in energy usage and billing
  • Vulnerability to data tampering and cyber-attacks
  • Difficulties in micro-level energy trading or peer-to-peer sharing

Disadvantages of Existing System

  • Environmental Impact: Fossil-fuel-based power plants contribute significantly to pollution and climate change.
  • Dependency on Non-Renewable Resources: Reliance on coal and oil, which are finite and depleting.
  • Infrastructure Costs: High setup and maintenance costs for plants and transmission lines.
  • High Energy Losses: Significant loss during energy transmission and distribution.

In summary: While current systems support various energy sources, they do not effectively utilize ambient noise. The proposed system aims to integrate noise-to-electricity conversion with blockchain technology to enable transparent, efficient, and decentralized energy management.

PROPOSED SYSTEM

The proposed system introduces an innovative approach to generate electricity from environmental noise using piezoelectric or acoustic-to-electric conversion systems, and leverages blockchain technology to manage and secure the energy data.

Numerous overlooked sources of sound — especially from industrial zones and public areas — can be tapped using transducers that convert these sound waves into usable electrical energy. This system effectively reimagines noise as a viable renewable resource.

Key Features

  1. Noise-to-Electricity Conversion: Captures industrial and public noise as energy through piezoelectric transducers.
  2. Alternative Energy Utilization: Demonstrates noise pollution’s potential as a clean energy source.
  3. NodeMCU Integration: Acts as the bridge, uploading energy metrics and booster circuit data to the cloud.
  4. Blockchain Technology: Secures and analyzes energy data in a decentralized and tamper-proof system.
  5. Efficient Storage & Monitoring: Energy is stored in batteries, while analytics tools process and archive performance data in the cloud.

Advantages of the Proposed System

  • Sustainable Energy Source: Noise is renewable and eco-friendly.
  • Decentralized Generation: Enables local production in noisy urban or industrial areas.
  • Low Environmental Impact: No harmful emissions in energy conversion.
  • Blockchain Transparency: Ensures trust, security, and accountability in data tracking.
  • Reduced Transmission Losses: Local generation cuts down long-distance energy loss.
  • Microgeneration Opportunities: Perfect for deployment in cities, factories, or transit hubs.
  • Smart Grid Integration: Compatible with smart infrastructure for optimal distribution and usage.
